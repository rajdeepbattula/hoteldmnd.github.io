Design Document- Hotel Booking Demand

Introduction

The goal of our visualization is to provide insight into what a hotel manager or owner (Hotelier) or owner might need from a hotel. We designed our analysis from 3 main aspects- People Behavior, Hotel Demands, as well as Country Analysis, and Market segmentation based on 2 main types of Hotels- City Hotels and Resort Hotels.

People's Behavior  - describes categories of people such as adults, babies, and children, Repeated Guests versus new guests, and Room assignment based on booking.

Hotel Demands - show the difference between City and Resort Hotels. Cancellations versus booked.

Country Analysis and Market Segmentation -  reveals where customers are coming from and how they tend to make their bookings and meal types offered.

Data Description

We used  a hotel booking dataset from Kaggle (Mostipak, J. 2020). The dataset has 32 columns with over 100,000 rows ranging from the year 2015-2017.  Data contains the type of hotels (City & Resort hotels) and other booking types preferred by people. The data is collected from different countries and can be analyzed in a better way to understand the overall ratio of bookings in different countries and people visiting them. Design Choices

We decided to use Tableau rather than D3 because it has drag-and-drop features and advanced features specific to visualizations and does not require an extensive amount of developer hours.

We initially created a dashboard for each aspect and approached the project from a user perspective. As a traveler, this information may be insightful to plan the next vacation. A traveler may want to know the most popular type of hotels, the amenities offered, prices as well as the best time or the most popular time to travel.

We first tried to work on designing the graphs individually to analyze the data and then modified some of the designs for the final dashboard. Following are the 3 different categories the dataset was divided into, and then we worked on it to create some initial design for the final dashboard.

To start with basics and to try on getting answers to each question, Initially, we prepared individual sheets for questions in the proposal. Later we merged multiple sheets into a single sheet to make the dashboard concise and clear.

We made various choices in picking the color scales for making the dashboard look vibrant and reader-friendly. We observed that there was too much information in each section which will require three dashboards to consume the information without creating clutter.

Design Outcome and Observations

Overall Approach

We landed on designing Hotel Booking Demand from the perspective of a Hotelier. Starting with a map, a Hotelier will understand customer reach and market segmentations. Followed by hotel characteristics, then into customer types and behavior. We leveraged the use of interactivities and tooltips are important to provide more context and explanation of other elements.

Furthermore, we leveraged the use of sequential, divergent, and quantitative color mappings. For example, Earlier we tried using quantitative and sequential mapping for the map and decided to use Divergent mapping in maps to indicate the percentage differences between different countries. The color-blind palette was used to show city versus resort hotels. Finally, we simulated our visualization with a color oracle and determined that a color blinded Hotelier will be able to understand the use of colors and graphs in the visualization.

People Behavior

  • People behavior analysis shows that most people book city hotels compared to resort hotels.
  • People tend to visit and book hotels most likely on weekdays and less on weekends. 
  • The ratio between adults, babies, and children shows that adults are the ones booking the hotels and their stay rate is more.
  • Based on guests, we also found that new guests visiting the hotels are not likely to book the hotels again.
  • Guests typically start arriving more months in March, April, and a higher number in May, also in August, and September, and mostly in October.
  • If we filter month & year wise then the percentage of guests visited more in September 2015, October 2016, and May 2017.
  • The analysis also shows the type of customers (group, transient, transient party) who have not paid the deposit money.

Hotel Characteristics

  • Our analysis shows that City Hotels are the most popular type of hotel compared to Resort Hotels.
  • The reservation status of checked-in with respect to canceled follows the same curve. The higher the check-in rate, the greater the cancellation rate, and vice-versa.
  • Booking hotels on weeknights are more popular than booking on weekend nights. Most guests booking hotels are new guests versus repeated guests.
  • Almost 10% of the people aren’t getting assigned to the same room that they reserved.
  • More children and babies arrive in July and August, So more childcare assistance might be needed for hotels to handle the children and babies.

Country Analysis and Market Segmentation

  • The diverging color in the Map visuals shows that Portugal has the most number of bookings by agents, the highest percentage of people bookings, and is also the most visited country.
  • People generally book city hotels when compared to the ratio of city hotels versus resort hotels using the stacked bars.
  • People are likely to opt for the Online TA market segment as compared to the others.
  • We can also analyze different types of meals offered by the hotels within different countries.
  • Booking distribution channels such as corporate, GDS, Direct, and TA can be identified based on the country filter, the general distribution channel used largely is TA/TO by most of the countries.
  • It is interesting to note that for direct bookings, Resort Hotels are more popular than City Hotels.

Limitations

  • It was challenging to perform collaboration designs. We were not able to merge our dashboard so we had to redesign one dashboard to contain all designs.
